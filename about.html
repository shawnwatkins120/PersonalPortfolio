About

I'm a Computer Science student at Old Dominion University (B.S., 2026) and a software engineer based in Hampton Roads, Virginia. My focus is building reliable, well-structured software across the full stack — from web applications and AI-driven services down to low-level embedded firmware — with an emphasis on clean architecture and maintainable, testable code.

I currently lead software development for an R&D team at MI Technical Solutions, building a walking robot for naval hull inspection. The work spans the entire system: Java application logic as a multi-module Maven project on a Raspberry Pi 5, motor-control and serial-communication firmware on Arduino microcontrollers, sensor integration over I2C, and real-time camera streaming. Alongside that, I'm building Keelson, a React and LLM application that turns field inspection evidence into submission-ready Navy maintenance reports with a reviewer kept in the loop at every step.

My background also includes network infrastructure and a Governance, Risk, and Compliance application from an IT internship with the City of Chesapeake, plus AI and full-stack projects ranging from a six-agent GPT-4o automation pipeline to a full-stack credibility-analysis app. I work primarily in Java, Python, and C#/.NET, and I'm comfortable in Agile teams with Git-based workflows and CI/CD.

I'm drawn to defense and government technology, and to problems where software has to hold up against real hardware and real-world constraints. I'm always looking for the next hard problem to build through.
